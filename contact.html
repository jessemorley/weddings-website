Hi, I'm Jesse. Nice to meet you.

I grew up in nipaluna (Hobart), raised on a diet of camping, chlorine and chamber music.

I first picked up a camera in my early twenties and quickly merged it with my other passion, cycling. I shot races, training camps, bikes and equipment, team and rider portraits, and apparel. I enjoyed the aesthetic challenge of producing more editorial-style images, but found I was still most drawn to the candid moments of unfiltered emotion. I wanted to tell stories. That realisation, paired with my obsession with love, made my path very clear. I was going to shoot weddings:

To be there for the coming together of family and friends to celebrate the love and lives of the people they care about.

It’s in vogue to speak about centering one’s photographic philosophy around capturing the spontaneous “in-between” moments—and while I agree that genuine unscripted moments are part of the magic of weddings, I also acknowledge that in order to get the shots that you want, you have to put the work in.

I believe my job as a photographer is to help provide a structure to your day that balances losing yourself in the magic of the event and setting up for truly beautiful images to remember it by.

I'm based in Sydney, but travel Australia wide, especially Victoria and my home state Tasmania. My books are open for 2024-2025, so let's talk!
